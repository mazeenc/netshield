Cyber Security Safety for Seniors

Welcome to our cyber security safety page designed to help seniors stay safe online.

Why is Cyber Security Important for Seniors?

Seniors are increasingly targeted by cyber criminals due to their potential vulnerability to online scams and lack of familiarity with technology. Understanding cyber security best practices is essential for protecting personal information, financial assets, and digital devices.

Common Cyber Threats Seniors Face

Protecting Your Personal Information

Follow these tips to safeguard your personal information online.

Passwords

Use a strong, unique password for each online account ensuring a harder way for others to access your account, keeping your account safe.

Two-Factor Authentication

Enable two-factor authentication whenever possible on each account, ensuring a more secure way of keeping information safe.

Social Media

Avoid sharing personal information on social media platforms in order to keep you and your account safe. Do not give your password to someone who is not verified or someone you don't trust.

Update Devices

Regularly update your devices and sotware to patch security vulnerabilities. Security features are always being updated by each new software you download.

Phishing emails often contain spelling or grammatical errors, request sensitive information, and use urgent language to prompt immediate action. Be wary of emails from unknown senders or unexpected messages from familiar contacts.

If you suspect a cyber attack, refrain from clicking on any links or downloading attachments in suspicious emails. Contact your bank or financial institution immediately if you believe your accounts have been compromised. Consider reporting the incident to the appropriate authorities or seeking assistance from a trusted IT professional.
