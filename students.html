Cyber Security Safety for Students

Welcome to our cyber security safety page designed to help students stay safe online.

During this stage of your academic and professional journey, your account holds sensitive data crucial for both present education and future career endeavors.
Learn how to safeguard your information and detect possible malware below.


1. How can I ensure that my personal information remains secure when using online educational platforms or submitting assignments electronically?

To keep your personal information secure on online educational platforms, use strong, unique passwords for each account and avoid sharing sensitive details unnecessarily. Be cautious when submitting assignments or participating in discussions, ensuring that you're using secure connections and reputable platforms. Familiarize yourself with the platform's privacy settings and adjust them as needed to control who can access your information.

2. What steps should I take to protect myself from cyberbullying or online harassment while engaging in virtual classrooms or socializing online with classmates?

Protect yourself from cyberbullying and online harassment by being mindful of what you share online and who you interact with. If you encounter harassment or bullying, report it to the appropriate authorities or platform moderators. Consider using privacy settings to limit who can contact you or view your profile, and don't hesitate to reach out to trusted adults or counselors for support.

3. Are there specific precautions I should take when downloading educational resources or software from the internet to avoid malware or viruses?

When downloading educational resources or software from the internet, only use reputable sources and verify the legitimacy of the download before proceeding. Be cautious of pop-up ads or suspicious links, and consider using antivirus software to scan downloads for malware or viruses before opening them.

4. How can I recognize and avoid online scams targeting students, such as fake scholarship offers or fraudulent job postings?

Be skeptical of unsolicited offers, especially those promising scholarships or job opportunities that seem too good to be true. Research the organization or company offering the opportunity, and never provide personal or financial information without verifying the legitimacy of the offer first. If in doubt, consult with school counselors or trusted advisors for guidance.

5. What measures should I implement to safeguard my online accounts and passwords, especially when accessing school-related platforms or services from multiple devices?

Safeguard your online accounts and passwords by using strong, complex passwords that include a mix of letters, numbers, and special characters. Avoid using easily guessable information such as your name or birthdate. Consider using a password manager to securely store and manage your passwords across multiple devices. Enable two-factor authentication whenever possible for an extra layer of security. Regularly update your passwords and monitor your accounts for any suspicious activity.
